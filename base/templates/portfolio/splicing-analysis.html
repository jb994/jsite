  Differential Splicing Analysis


Gene splicing is the process by which cells combine exons and introns into fully functioning genes. Some genes can be expressed in many differnent forms and with varying functions based on how their exons are combined together. As a result in certain cases it is useful to understand the ways genes can be compiled into their finished states and the ways this process alters cell and tissue function.


No single software for performin differentioal splicing anlaysis has risen to be the most effective or industry standard for the process. Additionally, Mehmood et al. in their systematic evaluation of various tools in the field found there was a large amount of variability in the conclusions drawn depending on which tool you use. As a result, it is useful to try multiple softwares and to look for results which overlap between them or which confirm knowledge previous found in literature on the topic.




Using the information about which exons are differentially expressed one can then cross-reference it back to known information about the different isoforms which can result from the splicing to get a better idea of exactly what effect the difference in splicing could be causing.
