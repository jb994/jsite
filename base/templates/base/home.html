Bioinformatician & Machine Learning Engineer

Having completed my Masters in Computer Science specialized in Artificial Intelligence in 2022 from the University of Freiburg, Germany, I began working as a bioinformatics consultant using my knowledge of single-cell RNA sequencing tools to analyze the gene expression profiles of new drugs as they move through clinical trials. Currently I am Lead Bioinformatician for the Translational Oncology Research Center at the Vrije Universiteit Brussel (VUB).

I've worked with 4 seperate organizations doing various analyses ranging from differential gene expression detection for both bulk- and single-cell RNA seq data, investigating chromatin accesibility, identifying key mutations in variant calling format data, performing differential splicing analyses to understand genes affected by alterations to the spliceosome, as well as other tasks related to biological data analysis. More recently, I've been developing machine learning models to determine the key features involved in severe fibrotic liver tissues and the features involved in the shrinking of tumors given drug treatment. These models work to learn the most important relationships between genes in order to understand how novel drugs can reduce fibrosis and cancer.


Experience & Education

I completed my Bachelors in Mathematics in 2019 at the University of Alberta and my Masters in Computer Science from the University of Freiburg, Germany in 2022. Specialized in artificial intelligence, I've focused my education on the various machine learning models at the forefront of the field and how to refine them to be able to tackle any task. My thesis in particular was on developing a novel method for projecting high-dimensional single-cell time-series datasets in the field of bioinformatics to produce more meaningful embeddings.

I've worked at multiple bioinformatics labs throughout my studies and most recently have worked as a bioinformatics & machine learning consultant in pharmaceuticals analysing multi-omics data to discover the mechanisms of novel drugs in the treatment of non-fatty alcoholic liver disease and various cancers. Currently I work for the VUB's Translational Oncology Research Center in Brussels, Belgium.

What I Know

I'm fluent in Python and am knowledgeable in many of its packages including but not lmited to: Numpy, Pandas, Django, Scikit-learn, Tensorflow, Scanpy, etc., as well as the visualization packages Matplotlib, Seaborn, and Plotly.

I've worked extensively with R as it is the default programming language for most bioinformatics softwares such as DESeq, EdgeR, WGCNA, Seurat, etc. Additionally, I have some limited javascript experience.

My education was largely focused on artificial intelligence and has allowed me to accumulate a strong understanding of various machine learning techniques including regression, decision trees and their variants, deep-neural networks, convolutional neural networks, & generative adversarial networks.
